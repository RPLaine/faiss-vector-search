# Article Structure and Style

## Article Components

### Headline
- Clear and specific
- Active voice preferred
- Typically 5-10 words
- Avoid puns that obscure meaning
- Include key information or intrigue

### Subheadline (optional)
- Provides additional context
- 10-20 words
- Clarifies or expands on headline

### Byline
- Author name and credentials
- Date of publication
- Location if relevant

### Lead Paragraph
- Summary of the story (nut graf)
- 1-3 sentences
- Answers primary W's
- Sets tone for piece

### Body Paragraphs
- One main idea per paragraph
- Short paragraphs (2-4 sentences)
- Use transitions between ideas
- Include quotes to add voices
- Provide evidence and examples

### Conclusion
- Summarize or provide closure
- Look forward or provide context
- Avoid introducing new information
- End with impact or resonance

## Writing Style Guidelines

### Voice and Tone
- Active voice: "The mayor announced..." not "It was announced by the mayor..."
- Present tense for current events
- Past tense for completed actions
- Third person for news reporting
- First person only in opinion/feature pieces

### Clarity and Conciseness
- Short, simple sentences
- Avoid jargon and technical terms
- Define acronyms on first use
- One idea per sentence
- Eliminate unnecessary words

### Attribution
- Attribute opinions and claims
- "According to..." or "Smith said..."
- Place attribution at end of quote
- Avoid "states," "claimed," "admitted" (loaded verbs)
- Use "said" as default attribution

### Quotes
- Use direct quotes for unique language or opinions
- Paraphrase for routine information
- Never alter quotes (use [...] for omissions)
- Introduce quote with context
- Alternate between direct quotes and paraphrase

## Common Style Points
- Numbers: Spell out one through nine, use numerals for 10+
- Dates: Month Day, Year (January 15, 2024)
- Time: Use numerals with a.m./p.m.
- Titles: Capitalize before names, lowercase after
- Abbreviations: FBI, CIA, UN (no periods for well-known organizations)
