# Interview Techniques

## Preparation
Before the interview:
- Research your subject thoroughly
- Prepare open-ended questions
- Understand the context and background
- Have backup questions ready
- Test recording equipment

## Types of Questions
### Open-ended questions
Questions that cannot be answered with yes/no:
- "Tell me about..."
- "How did you feel when..."
- "What was going through your mind..."
- "Describe the situation..."

### Follow-up questions
- "Can you elaborate on that?"
- "What happened next?"
- "Why do you think that occurred?"

### Challenging questions
- Present contradictory information
- Ask for clarification on vague statements
- Request specific examples

## Interview Best Practices
- Build rapport before asking tough questions
- Listen actively and take notes
- Allow silence - it encourages elaboration
- Ask for permission before recording
- Verify spellings and titles
- Follow up via email for clarification

## Ethical Considerations
- Identify yourself as a journalist
- Clarify on/off the record status
- Protect source confidentiality when promised
- Be transparent about your intentions
- Respect trauma and sensitivity

## After the Interview
- Transcribe important quotes accurately
- Verify facts and figures
- Contact subject to confirm complex information
- Thank the interviewee
- Store notes securely
